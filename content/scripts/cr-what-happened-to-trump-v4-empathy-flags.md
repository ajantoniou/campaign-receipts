## Flags

### FLAG-01 — ASSUMES_CONTEXT_VIEWER_DOESNT_HAVE
- **Beat:** 5
- **Line:** "It was called the J.C.P.O.A. The Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action."
- **Issue:** The acronym lands before the human reason the deal existed. A viewer who has never heard "J.C.P.O.A." gets the letters, then the long English name, then the purpose — three cognitive steps before the felt stakes. The very next sentence redeems it ("The whole job of that deal was to make a war with Iran unnecessary") but the acronym-first ordering still front-loads jargon over meaning.
- **Suggested fix DIRECTION:** Lead with what the deal DID for the viewer ("There was a deal whose whole job was to make a war with Iran unnecessary"), then name it second. Acknowledgment of the human stake before the acronym. Per founder memory `feedback_aipac_pronunciation.md`, keep the dotted spelling for TTS — just reorder.

### FLAG-02 — TALKS_DOWN
- **Beat:** 5
- **Line:** "In plain English: stop using the government to punish people for speaking up."
- **Issue:** Actually this line is in Beat 6, not 5 — let me restate. The phrase "In plain English" implies the prior line wasn't plain — which means the writer just put a not-plain line in front of a 3rd-grade viewer and is now translating it for them. That's the talk-down move: writer signals "I know that was hard, let me dumb it down." The viewer hears it.
- **Beat:** 6
- **Line:** 'In plain English: stop using the government to punish people for speaking up.'
- **Suggested fix DIRECTION:** Cut the meta-frame. Just say the plain-English version. The verbatim promise can land first, the human translation can follow as its own sentence without announcing itself as a translation. Trust the viewer to bridge the two.

### FLAG-03 — ANSWER_BEFORE_ACKNOWLEDGMENT
- **Beat:** 4
- **Line:** "That's the gap. Not a lie. Not a flip. A gap. The page set the rule. Then the same hand decided the rule didn't apply."
- **Issue:** The "not a lie / not a flip / a gap" sequence is the writer pre-empting the viewer's anger before the viewer has been given room to feel it. It tells the 2024 voter what to feel ("don't call it a lie") before acknowledging that calling it a lie would be the human response. The disappointment lands harder if the viewer is allowed to arrive at "gap" themselves rather than being instructed.
- **Suggested fix DIRECTION:** Acknowledge the human response first ("You could call it a lie. A lot of people have."), then offer the cooler frame ("What the record shows is a gap"). Same destination, but the viewer gets to be a grown-up about it instead of being told what label to use.

### FLAG-04 — DISCLAIMER_AS_DEFLECTION
- **Beat:** 7
- **Line:** "Nobody is telling you the vote was wrong. Nobody is telling you to flip."
- **Issue:** Two consecutive "nobody is telling you" disclaimers in back-to-back sentences read as ass-covering. One lands as respect; two reads as the writer worrying the viewer is about to close the tab. The repetition signals distrust. Cincinnati Mom would clock the second one.
- **Suggested fix DIRECTION:** Keep one, cut the other. The stronger of the two is "Nobody is telling you to flip" because it names the specific action the viewer is afraid of being pushed toward. Let that line carry the weight alone. The follow-up sentence ("The page was the deal you were offered. You read it. You voted on it.") already does the "you weren't wrong" work without needing the disclaimer to set it up.

### FLAG-05 — TALKS_DOWN (mild)
- **Beat:** 3
- **Line:** "Go to donaldjtrump.com today. Look for the words you voted on. You will not find them."
- **Issue:** "You will not find them" is correct but slightly oracular — the writer telling the viewer what they will or won't experience. The viewer hasn't gone to the site yet. The line works better as an invitation than a prediction.
- **Suggested fix DIRECTION:** Soften to invitation: "Go look. The words aren't there." Same fact, but the viewer is the one discovering it, not being told what they'll discover. Smaller note than the others — only flag because the rest of Beat 3 ("the campaign site does not remember") nails the invitation tone and this line breaks the pattern.
