# sealed-aipac-embassy-v2 — VO v4 (MrBeast packaging pass)

---

## BEAT 1 — You remember this day (hook acknowledgment)

**VO:** On May fourteenth, 2018, a promise was kept. The same afternoon, sixty people died — and you were only shown one half.

You remember the day the embassy moved. Maybe you picked a side that afternoon. That's fair.

[pause:400ms]

But here is the strange part. You only remember half of it.

One spring afternoon. Two things happened. You saw one on the news. You moved on.

That was seven years ago. The other half was on the same page.

---

## BEAT 2 — For twenty years, four presidents said: not yet

First, how we got here.

For twenty years, four presidents said one thing: not yet.

A law from nineteen ninety-five let any president pause the move. He just signed a paper every six months.

So every president signed it. Clinton signed it. Bush signed it. Obama signed it.

Then one president stopped. And what they declined, somebody signed.

---

## BEAT 3 — December sixth: the capital was recognized

December sixth, two thousand seventeen. The first half landed on the record.

The United States said Jerusalem was the capital. It was signed. It was dated. It was real.

But a signature is not a building. The building came five months later.

---

## BEAT 4 — May fourteenth: the ribbon was cut

May fourteenth, two thousand eighteen. The ribbon was cut. The embassy opened in Jerusalem.

This was a top AY-pack priority. A top A-I-P-A-C priority.

In other words, a group in Washington had wanted this for years. That group pays for ads to push laws.

On this date, the promise and the priority lined up. One date. One ribbon.

This is the half you remember. But the date carried more.

---

## BEAT 5 — The same afternoon (why this matters)

The same afternoon, sixty people died at the Gaza border.

These were real people. They died on a real day. It was the same date as the ribbon.

The number is the receipt. We are not here to tell you what to feel.

One afternoon held both. A ribbon in Jerusalem. A count at the border.

You saw one half for seven years. The other half was on the front page.

---

## BEAT 6 — Five months earlier, the world had counted

Now, was anyone keeping a tally? Yes. And it came earlier than you think.

Five months earlier, the world had already recorded its count.

In December, the UN voted on the new capital. The count was one hundred twenty-eight to nine.

That vote was about the December decision. It was not about this May afternoon.

But the number is public. It is part of the story.

So the date had a ribbon. It had a toll. And a vote was already on the books.

---

## BEAT 7 — The book grades it KEPT (cold-close warmth)

So how does the book grade it?

The SEALED book grades this promise number seventy-four. KEPT.

That grade is narrow. It grades the move itself. It does not grade the afternoon.

The promise was kept. The same afternoon, sixty people died.

Both are documented. Both share one date.

Here is what this afternoon taught us. A promise can be kept. And it can still share a date with the rest of the story.


You are allowed to hold both. Nobody tells you what to feel.

You just deserve the whole afternoon.
