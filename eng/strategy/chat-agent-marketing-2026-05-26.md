# Chat Agent Marketing — 2026-05-26

Two-specialist brief on the $9/mo Political Donor Intelligence Layer launch.

---

## Sales decision

**Call: DO NOT put the full v1→v2→v3 scope ladder in public marketing copy. Lead with v1 coverage as a complete, confident promise. Reveal the roadmap one tier down — on the pricing page or in a "Coverage" link — and use v2/v3 as re-engagement beats.**

Reasoning (Sales & Partnership lens):
1. Hero copy that lists what's *missing* reads "beta" — kills $9/mo trust at first glance. Buyers pay for what works today, not a Gantt chart.
2. "Federal + governors + major mayors" is already a TAM most buyers can't exhaust in a year. Disclosing the ceiling shrinks perceived value.
3. Every roadmap promise becomes a sales objection ("when does my state senator land?"). I'd rather field "do you cover X?" and answer truthfully than defend a public timeline I don't control.
4. Re-engagement leverage is real: each tier expansion = a launch email, a PR beat, a churn-saver. Burn that powder in tranches, not on day one.
5. Differentiation vs. ChatGPT comes from depth of *current* coverage + cited receipts, not from a future-tense roadmap. ChatGPT can also promise things.
6. Compromise: a single line on the pricing/FAQ page — "Coverage expands quarterly; state legislators next." Anchors ambition without bloating the hero.

---

## Marketing copy

Constraints applied: ≤25 words total per variant, ≤15 words per sentence, verb-led, plain English (no "intelligence layer," "donor influence"), one verb-led CTA.

### 5 variants (ranked)

**#1 — WINNER**
> See who paid your politician. Ask anything. Get receipts in seconds.
> **Start digging — $9/mo**

**#2**
> Type a politician's name. We show you every donor, every vote they bought, every bill they shaped.
> **Search a name — $9/mo**

**#3**
> Follow the money behind any federal lawmaker, governor, or big-city mayor. Chat, ask, cite, share.
> **Try it for $9**

**#4**
> Ask: who paid for this vote? We answer with FEC filings, video clips, and timestamps.
> **Ask a question — $9/mo**

**#5**
> Chat with a bot that knows every donor check to Congress, governors, and mayors. Citations included.
> **Open the chat — $9/mo**

### Why #1 wins

It clears the 3rd-grade bar cleanest: three short verb-led sentences, zero abstractions, and the strongest emotional verb in the set ("paid your politician" makes it personal, not academic). It promises a *behavior* ("ask anything") and a *proof artifact* ("receipts") in nine words combined — that's the whole product. Variants #2 and #4 are close but front-load mechanics ("type a name," "ask"), which describes the UI instead of the payoff. #3 leaks scope ("federal lawmaker, governor, or big-city mayor") which violates the Sales decision above. #5 leads with "chat with a bot" — bots are a commodity; receipts are not. #1 also gives the cleanest CTA: "Start digging" is a verb the buyer wants to do, not a feature the product offers.
