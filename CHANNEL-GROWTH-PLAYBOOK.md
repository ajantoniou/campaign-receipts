## 1. The core bet, honestly

Yes, "popular money-in-politics YouTube channel" is achievable with this setup —
but not because the content is good. It's achievable because the **cheap pipeline
breaks the math that kills everyone else in this genre.** A human-run
campaign-finance channel makes 1 video a week and dies waiting for one to hit.
You can make 16 and let the algorithm pick the winner. That's the whole bet:
**we buy more lottery tickets than anyone, faster, on the news cycle.**

Honest base rate: most channels in this genre never pop. More Perfect Union and
Second Thought are survivorship — for every one, dozens flatlined under 1k subs.
Right now we are PRE-SIGNAL: 0 subs, 0 traction, the algorithm has nothing to
pattern-match. Nothing we ship "should" work yet — that's normal, not failure.

Realistic timeline: **first signs of life at 8–12 videos in** (one video gets
~10x the channel's median, CTR on something clears 6%). Subscriber inflection
(0 → first few hundred off one breakout) is a **week-6-to-10** event *if* it
happens. First 1k subs: optimistically 3 months, realistically 4–6. Anyone
promising faster is guessing.

## 2. Topic selection — the single biggest lever

Topic > thumbnail > everything else. A perfect thumbnail on a topic nobody
searches dies. The pipeline's news-speed is the weapon: **be the first
nonpartisan FEC-grounded explainer on a money story that's ALREADY trending.**

**The rule for picking the next 10 topics — score each idea 0–2 on three axes,
ship anything that scores 5+:**

1. **Already-rising demand (0–2).** Is the underlying story trending THIS WEEK?
   Check: Google Trends (rising queries), the r/politics front page, what
   OpenSecrets / FollowTheMoney / Popular Information / the AP wire are covering,
   YouTube search autocomplete for the politician's name. If people are already
   typing it, we ride it. If we'd have to *create* the demand, score 0.
2. **Collision / number (0–2).** Is there a clean VS-collision and a hard dollar
   figure? "$39M LOST vs WON 64%" is a 2. A vague "dark money in politics" essay
   is a 0. Every winning topic has a villain, a number, and an outcome.
3. **Evergreen tail (0–2).** Will this still get searched in 6 months (a sitting
   senator, a recurring donor like a megadonor PAC) — or is it dead in a week?

**Chase:** contested primaries with a spending blowout, a megadonor who bet big
and LOST, a sudden FEC filing that contradicts a politician's public line, a PAC
named after something wholesome that funds the opposite. **Avoid:** explainers
with no named person, "the system is broken" think-pieces, anything where we'd be
the 5th channel to cover it 3 days late, and anything that reads as partisan
(it nukes our one moat: "we follow the money, not the party").

Maintain a **live ranked backlog** of 20 scored ideas; refresh it every Monday.
The pipeline is fast enough that the bottleneck is *picking*, not *making*.

## 3. Cadence & volume

**3 long-form per week for 6 weeks = 18 videos before judging ANYTHING.**
This is the entire point of the cheap pipeline — use the volume advantage or
you've thrown away your only edge. At $10–30/video that's ~$360 max for the whole
test window, inside the $500 cap.

Rules:
- Two of every three should ride a live news story (news-speed); one can be an
  evergreen "receipt" (a sitting senator's donor map) for the search tail.
- **Never break the upload rhythm.** A consistent Mon/Wed/Fri at the same hour
  trains both the algorithm and the (future) audience. Same-day on a breaking
  story beats a polished video three days late.
- Do NOT redesign the format mid-test. 18 videos at one format = a clean signal.
  Changing the format every video = noise you can't read.

## 4. Thumbnail / title / first-3-seconds = the real product

**60% of human effort goes here, 40% into the video itself.** Per MrBeast: CTR
and the first minute decide everything; production polish is a distant third.
The pipeline already makes the video cheaply — the scarce resource is your
attention, and it belongs on the click + the first 3 seconds.

The VS-collision on the Paxton thumbnail (TEXAS / $39.3M / DONOR LOST vs PAXTON
WON 64%) is the pattern. **Systematize it into a locked template:**
- Left side: the money (big $ figure, the donor/PAC).
- Right side: the outcome (WON / LOST / %).
- A collision word or arrow between them. 3–5 words MAX on the image.
- Title states the number + the twist: "$39 Million Tried to Beat Paxton. It Lost."

**First 3 seconds = pay off the thumbnail immediately.** No logo intro, no "hey
guys." Open on the exact number and the exact result the thumbnail promised
("Thirty-nine million dollars. One Senate seat. The money lost."). The #1 cause
of death is the first minute — front-load the answer, then explain how.

For every video, generate **3 thumbnail/title pairs** and pick the most extreme
honest one. This is a thumbnail-designer subagent task, not an orchestrator call.

## 5. Shorts funnel

Shorts are the genre's main discovery engine now — they're how a 0-sub channel
gets its first cold reach. **Author 2 Shorts per long-form** (don't carve raw
windows — that cuts off mid-sentence with no punchline; write each from the LF
story with its own hook + payoff). Portfolio rule: **text cards + music, NO
voiceover** — VO gets cut off and reads as slop.

What makes a money-in-politics Short pop:
- **One number, one twist, under 8 seconds to the hook.** "A PAC spent $39M to
  beat this man. He won by 28 points." Card by card.
- End on a punchline or a clean WTF, not a CTA. The comment engine is the goal —
  a Short that makes people argue in the comments gets pushed.
- Pin a comment linking the long-form. Shorts viewers rarely sub; the job is to
  send the curious ones to the LF where the real watch-time + subscribe lives.

Ship Shorts within hours of the LF so they ride the same news wave.

## 6. The metrics that matter

Watch these in YouTube Studio, **first-24-hour numbers** for apples-to-apples:

- **CTR (click-through).** The single most important early number. Pre-signal,
  expect 2–4%. **6%+ on any video = the topic+thumbnail worked — make more like
  it.** Under 2% consistently = the topic or thumbnail is wrong, not the video.
- **AVD / AVP (avg view duration / %).** Did they stay? Aim for 40%+ AVP on a
  6–8 min video. A cliff in the first minute = fix the hook, not the back half.
- **Traffic source.** This is the tell. **Browse + Suggested rising = the
  algorithm is pushing you (the goal).** All-Search = you have a tail but no
  push yet. Shorts feed = the funnel is working.
- **Sub velocity.** Subs-per-1000-views. Pre-signal it's ~0. First video that
  converts at 1%+ is your signal of a format people want to follow.

**"This is working" by week 6:** at least one video over ~5k views with 6%+ CTR
and rising Browse traffic, plus net-positive sub velocity. **"Kill / pivot" by
week 8:** 18 videos shipped, median under a few hundred views, CTR stuck under
2%, zero Browse push. If that's the picture, the *topic lane or the format* is
wrong — change the lane, don't just keep shipping the same thing.

Banned reasoning (per founder's A/B rule spirit): never call it working off one
metric. CTR + AVD + traffic-source + sub-velocity together, or it's noise.

## 7. The 1-week + first-month action plan (cheap-first, sequenced)

**Week 1 (cheapest first — no new spend until the backlog exists):**
1. Build the **scored 20-idea backlog** (Section 2 rule). Half news-riding, half
   evergreen receipts. This is the highest-leverage hour of the month.
2. Lock the **VS-collision thumbnail template** + the 3-second hook formula as a
   reusable spec the pipeline pulls from every time.
3. Pull the Paxton video's first-24h numbers (CTR, AVD, traffic source). It's the
   only real data we have — read it, don't guess.
4. Ship **3 long-form** off the top of the backlog, Mon/Wed/Fri. 2 Shorts each.

**Weeks 2–6:**
5. Hold **3 LF/week**, same rhythm, same format. Don't redesign. 18 total.
6. Every Monday: refresh the backlog against what's trending that week; re-rank.
7. Track the 24h dashboard per video in one running sheet. Look for the FIRST
   video that clears 6% CTR — that topic+thumbnail combo is the pattern to mine.

**End of week 6 — the read:**
8. Score the 18 against Section 6. Double down on whatever lane produced the
   breakout (more videos in that topic family, that thumbnail style). Kill the
   lanes that got zero Browse push. Then decide: scale, pivot the lane, or
   escalate a kill review with the Chief Accountant.

The discipline that wins this: **don't fall in love with any one video, fall in
love with the volume.** Ship 18, let the algorithm vote, mine the winner.
