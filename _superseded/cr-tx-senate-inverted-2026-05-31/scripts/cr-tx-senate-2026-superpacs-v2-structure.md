## Scene 1 — Hook
**VO:** "If you live in Texas, you have seen the ads. You may feel like you can not keep up. So here is what we found. Nearly ninety million dollars is already picking your next senator. Most of it was spent before you ever voted."

## Scene 2 — Stakes / context
**VO:** "This is the race for one Texas seat in the U.S. Senate. Right now it is the most expensive Senate race in the country. The strange part is the money. Most of it does not come from the people running. It comes from outside groups called super PACs. In other words, a super PAC is a group that raises huge checks and buys ads. It just can not hand the money to a campaign. So the names on the ads are not the candidates. They are the groups. And the groups picked names that sound like you. But who is really behind those names? Stay with me."

## Scene 3 — The name game (Receipt 1)
**VO:** "Look at the biggest one. It is called Texans for a Conservative Majority. The name sounds like a room full of your neighbors. So we pulled the filing. Every super PAC must tell the government who pays it. That filing is public. It is free to read. And the truth is not a crowd. It is mostly one man. His name is John Nau. He sells beer, and he is a billionaire. He put in about fifteen point six million dollars. A Wall Street boss named Stephen Schwarzman added four million more. So a name that says Texans is really three rich men. That group has spent twenty-four million dollars. All of it against one Republican, Ken Paxton."

## Scene 4 — The pattern (Receipt 2)
**VO:** "Then it happens again. Another group is called Truth and Courage. It has spent about twenty-two million dollars against the Democrat, Colin Allred. Behind the friendly name is mostly one out-of-state billionaire, Jeff Yass. Same trick. A warm name on the outside. One giant check on the inside."

## Scene 5 — Mid turn: not just one party (Receipt 3)
**VO:** "Now here is the part people get wrong. They think only one side does this. They are wrong. There is a group called Lone Star Rising. It sounds like proud Texas folks. But the money is liberal money. A lot of it comes from a tech billionaire in California named Reid Hoffman. And it is spending against a Democrat, Jasmine Crockett, inside the Democrats own race. So both sides hide behind friendly names. That is the real story."

## Scene 6 — Why it matters (voter pivot)
**VO:** "Think about who these candidates are. Ken Paxton and Colin Allred want to speak for thirty million Texans in Washington. And the loudest voices in that fight are not Texans at all. They are a few billionaires you never met. They are spending money you were never meant to trace."

## Scene 7 — The hidden ones (honest gap)
**VO:** "Some of it we still can not see. One group is called WinSenate. It spent ten million dollars to help Allred. But its filing does not name its donors yet. Another group spent almost five million against Wesley Hunt. All of that came through one out-of-state pass-through. So we will tell you the honest thing. We do not know those names yet. The filing does not show them. And we will not make a name up."

## Scene 8 — Verdict
**VO:** "So here is the punchline. The friendly names are a costume. Texans for a Conservative Majority is one beer billionaire. Truth and Courage is one hedge-fund billionaire. The warm name is the disguise. It is not the message. And the only place the real face is written down is the public filing."

## Scene 9 — Cold close
**VO:** "Here is what this race taught us. The next ad will sound like your neighbors. It will not be. But the receipt that names who really paid is public. It is free. And it is yours. The full receipt and our free newsletter are linked below."
