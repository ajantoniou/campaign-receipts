## HOOK
**VO:** If you live in Texas, you have seen the ads. And you may feel like you can not keep up with all of them. Here is what we found. Nearly ninety million dollars is already trying to pick your next senator. And most of it was spent before you ever voted.

## CONTEXT
**VO:** This is the race for one Texas seat in the United States Senate. It is the most expensive Senate race in the country right now. The strange part is the money. Most of it does not come from the people running. It comes from outside groups called super PACs. In other words, a super PAC is a group that can raise huge amounts of money and spend it on ads, as long as it does not hand the money to the campaign. So the names on those ads are not the candidates. They are the groups. And the groups picked names that sound like you.

## THE NAME GAME
**VO:** Look at the biggest one. It is called Texans for a Conservative Majority. The name sounds like a crowd of your neighbors. So we pulled the filing. Every super PAC has to tell the government who pays. That filing is public, and it is free to read. The truth is not a crowd. It is mostly one man. His name is John Nau. He sells beer for a living, and he is a billionaire. He put in about fifteen point six million dollars. A Wall Street boss named Stephen Schwarzman added four million more. So a name that says Texans is really three rich men. That group has spent twenty-four million dollars, all of it against one Republican, Ken Paxton.

## THE PATTERN
**VO:** Then it happens again. Another group is called Truth and Courage. It has spent about twenty-two million dollars against the Democrat, Colin Allred. Behind the friendly name is mostly one out-of-state billionaire, Jeff Yass. Same trick. A warm name on the outside. One giant check on the inside.

## NOT JUST ONE PARTY
**VO:** Now here is the part people get wrong. They think this is only one side doing it. It is not. There is a group called Lone Star Rising. It sounds like proud Texas folks. But the money is liberal money, and a lot of it comes from a tech billionaire in California named Reid Hoffman. And it is spending against a Democrat, Jasmine Crockett, inside the Democrats own race. So both sides hide behind friendly names. That is the real story here.

## WHY IT MATTERS
**VO:** Think about who Colin Allred and Ken Paxton are for a second. They are the people asking to speak for thirty million Texans in Washington. And the loudest voices in that fight are not Texans at all. They are a few billionaires you never met, spending money you were not supposed to trace.

## THE HIDDEN ONES
**VO:** Some of it we still can not see. One group, called WinSenate, spent ten million dollars to help Allred. But its filing does not name the people behind it yet. Another group spent almost five million against Wesley Hunt, and all of that money came through a single out-of-state pass-through. So we will tell you the honest thing. We do not know those names yet. The filing does not show them. And we will not make a name up.

## VERDICT
**VO:** So here is the punchline. The friendly names are a costume. Texans for a Conservative Majority is one beer billionaire. Truth and Courage is one hedge-fund billionaire. The warm name is the disguise, not the message. And the only place the real face is written down is the public filing.

**VO:** Here is what this race taught us. The next ad you see will sound like your neighbors. It will not be. But the receipt that tells you who really paid is public, it is free, and it is yours to read. The full receipt and our free newsletter are linked below.
