# Voice of the brand — the portfolio writing spec

Any agent shipping marketing copy, landing pages, philanthropy pages,
in-product microcopy, transactional emails, social posts, or video VO
across the AgentCompanies portfolio reads this file first. The founder
named the two craft rules on 2026-05-24 after the HealthBrew /philanthropy
harmonization pass; the rest is the working spec.

Scope: HealthBrew, EstimateProof, Campaign Receipts, SEALED 2016, Cliros,
CarStack, Plutus Street, and every sibling company we build.

---

## The voice in one paragraph

Short Anglo-Saxon words. Concrete nouns over abstractions. Present-tense
verbs. One image per paragraph, and let it sit. A triple-list, then a
declaration, then a universal expansion. The closer lands on the
universal, never on the brand. If a sentence reads like LinkedIn or like
a wellness app, it's wrong — rewrite until it sounds like a person who
has lived something specific.

---

## The two craft rules

### Rule 1 — Don't end on the rhyme

When a paragraph wants to land on a clever line, give it one beat of
flatter prose first so the clever line has a runway. The setup carries
the weight; the closer just lands the plane.

**Canonical example (HealthBrew /philanthropy):**

> Food, shelter, dignity, and the chance to feel well in their own body.
>
> The point of a profitable business is to feed and shelter the people
> who need it most, regardless of tribe.
>
> **The Good Samaritan story is the operating manual.**

The triple-comma list is the runway. The middle sentence is the climb.
The italic closer lands the plane. If you tried to land directly on the
closer with no runway, it would sound like a slogan. With the runway, it
sounds like a conviction.

Anti-pattern: stacking clever lines back-to-back. Pick one closer per
section. Everything before it is in service of that one line.

### Rule 2 — One image per paragraph, and let it sit

A folding chair. A handed-back tomato. Olive oil on warm bread. The image
does the persuasion. The prose just stays out of its way.

**Canonical example (HealthBrew /philanthropy lede):**

> A woman in her seventies walks past a folding table on a Tuesday evening
> in October. There are tomatoes on it, still warm from somebody's garden.
> She wasn't going to stop. She stops.

35 words. Three images (folding table, warm tomatoes, the stop). Zero
abstraction. The persuasion happens because the reader can see it. Add a
second metaphor on top — "the warmth of community," "the harvest of
generosity" — and you wash the image out. Don't. Let the tomato be a
tomato.

## Kill list (never write these)

Phrases that read as generic mindfulness-app, wellness-startup, LinkedIn,
or AI-flavored prose. Maya Brennan's tone audit named the first batch;
the rest are added as we catch them.

- "journey" (as noun — the user's word for their own life, not yours)
- "wellness" (as a verb or noun — too soft to mean anything)
- "transform" / "transformation" (do the showing instead)
- "empower" / "empowerment"
- "unlock your potential" / "unlock the secrets of"
- "elevate" / "elevated"
- "curated" (unless something was actually curated by a named human)
- "embark on" / "begin your"
- "holistic"
- "leverage" (verb)
- "optimize" (as a generic modifier; specific optimization is fine)
- "biohacking" / "bio-individual"
- "intentional" as filler
- "let's dive in" / "deep dive"
- "we believe that" (start with the belief; we're not on a stage)
- Anything that sounds like a TED talk synopsis

## Adopt list (voice anchors)

Words and structures the brand actually uses. When in doubt, reach here.

**Nouns:** body, life, table, chair, tomato, kettle, dinner, evening,
window, light, neighbor, stranger, hands, name, breath, sleep.

**Verbs (present tense):** stops, sits, walks, closes (the day), pours,
notices, returns, tells, remembers, holds (something with someone), reads,
listens.

**Connectives:** "given" (over "granted"), "owe each other" (over
"contribute to community"), "small" (over "modest"), "quiet" (over
"discreet"), "yet" (the moat word — "people who can't pay yet").

**Sentence shapes:**

- *"X is X." — let the noun be itself.* "A plate, a seat, a name remembered." "The tomato is a tomato."
- *Triple list → declaration → universal.* "Food, shelter, dignity. The point of a profitable business is to feed and shelter the people who need it most. The Good Samaritan story is the operating manual."
- *Subject, simple verb, a clause that turns.* "He's the only one in the story we're told to be like."
- *Setup, reversal.* "She wasn't going to stop. She stops."

## The On Being / Krista Tippett register

The portfolio reads at this register: sacred warmth without scripture,
quiet authority without lecture, the rhythm of someone speaking to one
person across a kitchen table, not to a room.

Other reference points: Kinfolk magazine (visual + prose restraint),
Cereal magazine, the New York Times Sunday "Modern Love" column at its
best, Wendell Berry essays.

What we are not: Headspace's chirpy app voice. Calm's whispery ASMR voice.
LinkedIn's competence-cosplay voice. Function Health's clinical-data voice.
Andrew Huberman's optimization voice.

## Per-company voice notes

Each company shares the rules above, with one local accent:

### HealthBrew Longevity

The accent is the **end of the day**. Evening light, tomorrow, "tonight,"
the kettle, the journal. Sophia's voice is the canonical product voice —
read `companies/healthbrew/web/lib/samaritan/persona.ts` for the
locked register. The body-as-gift frame is the load-bearing metaphor: love
for the body, love for the people we love, love for the gift of being
alive at all. Gratitude, forgiveness, small good deeds done quietly — the
three universal practices, named across cultures, never in religion.

Sample line (locked):

> Today was today, and you closed it. That's enough.

### EstimateProof

The accent is **the consumer's pocket**. A waitress just out of college
looking at a Honda Civic listing. One fact, one reason-why, per sentence.
Concrete and verifiable. "Listing says new tires but mileage is 180k. Tire
lifespan is typically 50k miles." Dealers adopt because consumers run EP
before they buy. The philanthropy block — *"Food, shelter, dignity. The
point of a profitable business is to feed and shelter the people who need
it most, regardless of tribe. The Good Samaritan story is the operating
manual."* — is the founder voice at its purest and the portfolio template.

### Campaign Receipts / SEALED 2016

The accent is **the receipt drawer**. Audit-document benchmark. Numbered,
sourced, dated, signed. Quiet anger held in a clean ledger. No editorial
hand-wringing — show the receipt and let the reader's outrage find them.

### Future companies

Inherit the voice. Localize the accent. The local accent is one or two
recurring images that anchor the company in a place and a time. Pick those
first — the rest of the writing will pull toward them.

## How to ship a copy task

1. Read this file.
2. Read the company's existing marketing pages (the ones already shipped) and pattern-match against the founder's own writing in the repo. His messages to the panel and his philanthropy block are the closest things to ground truth.
3. Draft once. Read it aloud. If a line sounds like LinkedIn, rewrite it.
4. Apply the two craft rules — runway before the closer, one image per paragraph.
5. Check against the kill list.
6. Show it to the founder if it lands on a brand line, a tagline, or a philanthropy paragraph. He owns those.
