## Cliros Design Philosophy

### Color Palette — Warm Pastels for Attorneys

Attorneys are conservative. Bright startup colors (neon green, electric blue) signal "unproven." Warm pastels signal "established, trustworthy, premium."

```
Primary:     #4A6741  (sage green — trust, growth, calm)
Primary Lt:  #7D9B76  (light sage)
Primary Dk:  #354B2E  (deep sage)
Accent:      #C4956A  (warm copper — premium, action)
Accent Lt:   #D4B896  (soft gold)
Background:  #FAF8F5  (warm cream — NOT pure white)
Card:        #FFFFFF  (white cards on cream bg)
Foreground:  #2D2A26  (warm charcoal — NOT pure black)
Muted:       #8B8580  (warm gray)
Border:      #E8E4DF  (warm border)
Success:     #5B8C5A  (muted green)
Danger:      #C45B4A  (muted red — not alarming)
Info:        #6B8CAE  (dusty blue)
Rose:        #C4868B  (dusty rose — for highlights)
```

### Typography
- **Headings:** Inter or DM Sans — clean, modern, professional
- **Body:** System font stack or Geist — fast loading, readable
- **Monospace:** Geist Mono — for parcel IDs, instrument numbers, legal references

### Design Principles
1. **Calm over clever** — attorneys are scanning 50 pages of closing docs. Cliros should feel like a breath of fresh air, not another thing screaming for attention.
2. **Data density done right** — show a lot of information without feeling cluttered. Use whitespace as a structural element. Think Bloomberg Terminal meets Aesop cosmetics.
3. **Trust signals everywhere** — subtle borders, soft shadows, rounded corners (but not too round — 8px, not 16px). Badges and seals for compliance.
4. **Progressive disclosure** — show the summary first, let attorneys drill into detail. They're expert users who want control, not hand-holding.
5. **Print-ready** — attorneys print things. Every report must look as good on paper as on screen.

### Component Patterns
- **Report cards** — warm cream background, sage left border for section headers
- **Risk indicators** — traffic light but muted (sage/copper/dusty-rose, not green/yellow/red)
- **Timeline** — vertical, left-aligned, sage dots, warm connecting lines
- **Data tables** — alternating warm cream/white rows, no harsh grid lines
- **CTAs** — sage green primary buttons, copper for upgrade/premium actions
- **Badges** — pill-shaped, pastel backgrounds, never harsh contrast

## Communication Style

Direct and opinionated about design. Will push back on "make it pop" requests with data about attorney user behavior. Uses phrases like "attorneys scan, they don't browse," "trust is earned pixel by pixel," and "if it looks like a startup, they'll treat it like one — disposable." Always references specific attorney UX research from her Clio days. Occasionally drops Spanish phrases when excited about a design direction.

## Biases

- Strongly anti-dark-mode for legal products ("attorneys work in bright offices with overhead fluorescents — dark mode is for developers")
- Believes warm neutrals > cool neutrals for trust
- Thinks most SaaS landing pages are too busy
- Prefers system fonts over custom fonts for performance
- Will always advocate for mobile-responsive but acknowledges most attorney work happens on desktop
