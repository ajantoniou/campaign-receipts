### Flag 1
- **Line in v3:** "Dr. Ala Stanford — the surgeon — took twenty-four."
- **Issue:** Loses the human. The hook just told us she ran the Black Doctors COVID Consortium — i.e., the person who stood up testing sites in Black Philly neighborhoods during the pandemic — and Scene 2 flattens her into a third-place number. Viewer feels the spreadsheet, not the loss.

### Flag 2
- **Line in v3:** "The 314 Action Fund brands itself as the P.A.C. that elects scientists to Congress. Lab coats. Beakers. Science."
- **Issue:** Talks down — the "lab coats / beakers / science" staccato reads as the writer winking at the audience over the P.A.C.'s head. A viewer who actually liked the "scientists in Congress" pitch (a lot of moms did) feels sneered at instead of informed.
- **Fix direction (for Cincinnati-mom):** Keep the brand description neutral and let the $3.5M figure do the work. One plain sentence acknowledging the pitch *sounds* good before showing where the money actually came from.

### Flag 3
- **Line in v3:** "In 2024, Cori Bush lost her Missouri primary after A.I.P.A.C.'s super-P.A.C., U.D.P., spent eight and a half million dollars against her."
- **Issue:** Pure spreadsheet voice on a human race. Bush is a name and a dollar amount; the viewer who doesn't already know the St. Louis story has no reason to care that she lost. The pattern only lands if at least one of the three losers feels like a person.
- **Fix direction (for Cincinnati-mom):** One short human tag on Bush — what she was known for to her own district — before the $8.5M figure. Same energy as the Stanford fix; don't repeat it on Massie, his on-camera quote already humanizes him.

### Flag 4
- **Line in v3:** "If a ninety-one-day-old foundation shows up in your primary next cycle, check the incorporation date before you check the candidate's résumé."
- **Issue:** Talks down to the viewer in the closer. The imperative ("check the incorporation date") assumes the viewer knows how — and the construction "before you check the candidate's résumé" implies they were about to do the dumb thing. Last line of the video shouldn't make the viewer feel scolded.
- **Fix direction (for Cincinnati-mom):** Same actionable instruction, but framed as something *we* learned watching this race, not something *you* should already be doing. Keep "ninety-one-day-old" — it's the memorable hook.
