## Receipt 1 — 1995 law allowing six-month embassy-move waivers

**Claim:** A 1995 law let any president waive the embassy move every six months; four presidents used it for ~20 years.

**Verdict:** PRIMARY — confirmed.

- **Statute:** Jerusalem Embassy Act of 1995, **Public Law 104-45**, enacted **November 8, 1995**.
- **Primary URL (statute text, GovInfo):** https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/PLAW-104publ45/html/PLAW-104publ45.htm
- **Primary URL (PDF, Congress.gov):** https://www.congress.gov/104/plaws/publ45/PLAW-104publ45.pdf
- **Statute citation:** 109 Stat. 398.
- **Waiver provision (quoted/confirmed from statute):** Beginning October 1, 1998, the President may suspend the limitation on funds "for a period of six months if he determines and reports to Congress in advance that such suspension is necessary to protect the national security interests of the United States," and may extend the suspension for an additional six-month period under the same standard.
- **Retrieval date:** 2026-05-28.
- **Discrepancy vs brief:** None on the statute. NOTE the brief's framing "four presidents used the waiver for ~20 years" (Clinton, Bush, Obama, Trump-early) is a characterization, not a statutory fact — the *waiver mechanism* is primary-sourced; the *count of presidents who signed waivers* is historically accurate but each individual semiannual waiver determination is a separate State Dept record. The script can safely say "every president from 1995 until 2017 signed the waiver"; if it asserts a specific number of waivers signed, Stage 6 should not need to harden the mechanism itself.
