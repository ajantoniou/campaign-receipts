# 2016 Promise Corpus Audit — 2026-05-19

**Author:** agent (CR engineering)
**Trigger:** Prior agent flagged that the "64 missing promises" premise
(implicit in `145 - 81 = 64`) was unverified. This audit checks the book
prose against the seed.

## TL;DR

- **Existing seed (`scripts/seed-trump-2016-cycle.json`):** 81 entries
  = 9 chapter overviews + 72 individual pledges.
- **Standalone individual-promise h4 entries in the book's 2016 cycle
  chapters (Ch. 1–9):** 72.
- **Gap between seed and book standalone entries:** 0.
- **The "145" figure in `SCORECARD_TOTALS`** is the **editorial verdict
  tally** the book publishes (46 KEPT + 51 PARTIAL + 40 BROKEN + 8
  READER = 145). It is **not** a count of standalone h4 promise entries.
  It rolls up sub-pledges mentioned inline in chapter prose (e.g., the
  Drain-the-Swamp chapter explicitly states the "swamp" brand
  "encompassed at least 18 distinct promises" — but only 9 of those
  appear as h4 headers; the other ~9 are graded inline as part of the
  chapter narrative). The same pattern repeats across chapters.
- **No standalone-page-worthy promises are missing.** Writing 64 new
  per-promise pages would require either (a) inventing standalone
  promises that the book does not break out, or (b) creating pages
  whose content would have to be hallucinated from inline prose that
  doesn't cleanly map to a single sub-promise. Both fail the
  no-inventions bar.

## Methodology

1. Counted every `<h4 class="entry-title">…</h4>` in
   `companies/concise-sealed/scripts/build-retail-pdf.mjs`.
   - Raw count: 88
   - Minus "Receipt N" narrative items inside chapter case studies
     (these are illustrative narrative receipts, not standalone
     promises): 15
   - Minus the dynamic Chapter 10 entries (`ch10_2024platform()`) which
     iterate over `artifacts/sealed-ch10-2024-commitments.json`
     (the **2024 cycle**, explicitly out-of-scope for the 2016 seed):
     these are generated from a separate corpus of 52 entries.
   - **Net 2016-cycle standalone promise h4 entries: 72.**

2. Normalized titles (lower-case, smart-quote / em-dash strips) and
   diffed against the seed's 72 `Individual pledge` entries. Result:
   **0 deltas** — every book h4 corresponds to a seed row and vice versa.

3. Cross-checked the "145" figure against three counts:
   - 9 chapter overviews + 72 individual pledges + 52 ch10 2024
     commitments = 133. Doesn't match.
   - Book scorecard verdict sum 46+51+40+8 = 145. Matches the editorial
     claim exactly.
   - Therefore the 145 is a verdict-tally rollup, not a structural count
     of headered promises. The roll-up includes sub-pledges discussed
     inline (e.g., Drain-the-Swamp's "at least 18 distinct promises"
     framing).

## What this means for SEO scope

The original plan called for ~64 new per-promise SEO pages with
~250-word receipt copy. That plan is rejected because:

1. There are no missing standalone promises in the book to back those
   pages.
2. The "250-word receipt detail" content would have to be either
   (a) hallucinated, (b) lifted from inline chapter prose that doesn't
   map 1:1 to a sub-promise, or (c) generated from a primary-source
   browse-and-summarize loop that is out of $0 budget scope.

## Flag for the founder

The book's `145` is correct as a verdict tally. The CR masthead and
`SCORECARD_TOTALS` should continue to surface 145 as the editorial total
— but anyone reading the codebase comment in `lib/sealed-promises.ts`
(lines 12–15) should know the 81-vs-145 delta is **not** "64 missing
sub-promises waiting to be page-ified." It is the difference between
"distinct headlined promises with their own h4" and "promise-instances
that the book grades, counting inline sub-pledges that share a heading
with another promise." A future expansion that adds inline sub-pledges
as their own pages would require deliberate editorial extraction — not
bulk generation.
