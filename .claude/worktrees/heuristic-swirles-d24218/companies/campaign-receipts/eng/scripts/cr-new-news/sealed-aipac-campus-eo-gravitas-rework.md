## NEW OPEN (replaces old paragraphs 1 + the front of the hook)

> People died so you could speak. [pause:600ms] In 1776, American colonists
> fought a war against a king. One thing they fought for was the right to
> speak against the crown. [pause:400ms] They wanted to say a hard thing out
> loud and not be punished. Some of them died for that. [pause:600ms] Years
> later, the country wrote it down. The First Amendment. The right to speak.
> People have fought and died to keep that right ever since. [pause:800ms]
> So when a line moves on what you can say, it matters. It was paid for in
> blood.
>
> [pause:800ms] On December 11, 2019, one order moved that line. And nobody
> voted on it. [pause:400ms] That same order kept a donor's promise and broke
> a voter's. He promised a campus where you could say more. The order made it
> less. One paper. [pause:400ms] Two ledgers. Which promise? [pause:300ms]
> Watch the order do both at once.

The third paragraph ("You wanted free speech on campus protected…") is the
old paragraph 2, kept verbatim as the seam into the unchanged middle.
