# Thumbnail brief — CR TX-Senate 2026 super PACs (LF v9)

**Title it pairs with:** `$110 Million Hit Texas. The Biggest Checks Lost. Paxton Won 64%.`

The title already carries the $110M total, the "lost" finding, and the 64%.
The thumbnail must NOT repeat those. It zooms in on the single biggest check
and shows it dying. Title + thumbnail combine into one thought: *the most money
in the country, and the single biggest check, both lost.*

## Text overlay (≤5 words)

Primary, huge: **$39.3M LOST**

- That is the single largest check in the race (the pro-Cornyn PAC's spend
  against Paxton). It is NOT in the title, so it adds information instead of
  repeating it.
- "LOST" rendered as a red audit stamp angled across the figure — the void /
  rejected-stamp treatment from the CR audit-document system, not flat type.
- No second line of words. The receipt does the rest.

## Visual / figures

- **Aesthetic:** CR "audit document / paper-receipt" benchmark. The whole frame
  is a real-looking campaign-finance receipt or itemized FEC line on aged off-white
  paper — perforated edge, monospace ledger type, a faint "FEC" / filing-ID header,
  hole-punch and staple shadow. Quiet anger held in a clean ledger.
- **Hero element:** one printed ledger line reading the disbursement —
  e.g. `IE AGAINST: PAXTON, KEN ........ $39,300,000` — with the big red
  **LOST** stamp slammed across it at ~15°, slightly off-register like a real
  rubber stamp (ink heavier on one edge).
- **Figure:** Ken Paxton's face, small, upper-right corner, as a Wikimedia
  photo treated to look paper-clipped or taped to the receipt (use the
  ken-burns/Wikimedia path — real political figure, no generative face). He is
  calm/neutral, NOT gloating; the receipt carries the verdict, not his expression.
  A small green check or "WON ~64%" handwritten-margin note beside him is
  optional and secondary — keep it tiny so it never competes with $39.3M LOST.
- Do not caricature. Same skepticism every direction; this is a ledger, not a dunk.

## Color treatment

- Base: aged paper off-white / manila (#F4EFE3-ish), warm ledger black for the
  printed figures, monospace.
- The ONE color hit: audit red (#C0271E-ish) for the **LOST** stamp — the only
  saturated element in the frame, so the eye lands on it instantly even at
  mobile-grid size.
- Optional thin green for the secondary "WON" margin note only; keep it
  desaturated so red stays dominant.
- High contrast, mobile-first: $39.3M must be legible as a thumbnail thumbnail
  (the tiny grid size), so the red stamp + the dollar figure are the two things
  that survive shrinking.

## Why this works

The number in the thumbnail (single biggest check) and the number in the title
(total + result) don't overlap — together they say "the most expensive race in
the country, and its single biggest check, both lost." The red void-stamp on a
clean ledger is the CR brand thesis in one image: show the receipt, let the
outrage find them.
