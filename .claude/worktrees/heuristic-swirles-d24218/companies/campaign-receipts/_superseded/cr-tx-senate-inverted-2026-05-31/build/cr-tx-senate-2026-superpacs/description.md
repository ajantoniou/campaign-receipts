Ninety million dollars is already trying to pick your next Texas senator. And it is hiding behind names that sound like your neighbors.

We pulled the public FEC filings on the most expensive U.S. Senate race in the country — TX Senate 2026 — and traced the "grassroots Texans" super-PAC ads back to the handful of billionaires who actually paid for them. The friendly name is the costume. The receipt is where the real face is written.

WHAT THE FILINGS SHOW
• Texans for a Conservative Majority (FEC C00542217): $24.1M spent against Ken Paxton — funded mostly by one man, beer-distribution billionaire John Nau (~$15.6M), plus Blackstone's Stephen Schwarzman ($4M).
• Truth and Courage PAC (FEC C00796045): $22M against Colin Allred — biggest NAMED donor we can see is Jeff Yass at $2M; most of it is not itemized.
• Lone Star Rising PAC (FEC C00918268): $8.5M against Jasmine Crockett — backed by California's Reid Hoffman ($1.5M). Liberal money, Texan-sounding name. Both sides wear the costume.
• WinSenate (FEC C00865444): $10.1M for Allred — donors NOT itemized yet.
• Conservative Texans PAC (FEC C00932707): $4.9M against Wesley Hunt — 100% through one out-of-state pass-through.

We do not name donors the filings do not name. We will not make a name up.
