## CHAPTER 3 REPLACEMENT — price receipts (Jan-Jun 2025)

The promise said prices would come down on day one. Here is what the government's own price-takers wrote down in the first six months.

**Groceries.** The Bureau of Labor Statistics tracks grocery prices in a number called the food-at-home index. In June 2025 that number was [2.4 percent higher than it was a year before](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_07152025.htm). In January 2025, the same index [rose 0.5 percent in one month](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_02122025.pdf). Groceries did not come down. They went up.

**Eggs.** The USDA writes down the wholesale price of a dozen large white eggs every week. On [January 24, 2025](https://lifesourcenaturalfoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/ams_3725.pdf), that price was $6.55 a dozen. On [June 20, 2025](https://esmis.nal.usda.gov/sites/default/release-files/73667q50n/9c67zm651/7d27br56c/AMS_3725.PDF), that price was $2.73 a dozen. Eggs did come down. But they only came down because the bird flu wave that spiked them in January 2025 ran its course. They did not come down because of a White House order on day one.

**Gas.** The Energy Information Administration writes down the price of a gallon of regular gas every Monday. The week of January 20, 2025, the national average was [$3.109 a gallon](https://www.eia.gov/dnav/pet/hist/LeafHandler.ashx?n=PET&s=EMM_EPMR_PTE_NUS_DPG&f=W). The week of June 23, 2025, the national average was [$3.213 a gallon](https://www.eia.gov/dnav/pet/hist/LeafHandler.ashx?n=PET&s=EMM_EPMR_PTE_NUS_DPG&f=W). Gas went up about ten cents a gallon, not down.

Two of three kitchen-table prices went up. The one that fell, fell because a virus stopped killing chickens, not because of a signature. The promise was broken on the timeline.
