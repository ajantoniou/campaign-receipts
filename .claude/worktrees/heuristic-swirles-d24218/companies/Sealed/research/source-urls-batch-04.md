## Tricky Cases Worth Flagging

### 1. Promise #69 — "End Visa Lottery" (STRUCTURAL_ANOMALY + WEAKLY VERIFIED)
This promise was not a 2016 campaign centerpiece. Trump called for ending the visa lottery in 2017 primarily as a reactive response to the October 31, 2017 NYC truck attack perpetrated by a lottery visa recipient — not from a pre-election policy position. The RAISE Act (Trump-endorsed, 2017) would have eliminated the lottery, but it never passed. Including this as a 2016 campaign pledge requires treating Trump's general "immigration overhaul" rhetoric as encompassing it. The BROKEN verdict is accurate (lottery survived the term), but the framing as a 2016 "promise" is weaker than most entries.

### 2. Promise #71 — "Appoint Pro-Life Justices / Overturn Roe" (VERIFIED, but timing nuance)
The promise was clearly made and clearly kept — Trump appointed three justices who joined the Dobbs majority. However, Roe was actually overturned in June 2022, during Biden's presidency. The SEALED book grades this KEPT based on the mechanism (appointments) being completed in Trump's term, which is defensible. But a strict grader could argue the outcome — Roe overturned — happened outside the graded term window. The book's KEPT verdict is editorially sound and the correct call.

### 3. Promise #80 — "Achieve Energy Independence" (VERIFIED, but contested definition)
The Wikipedia source fetched for this entry notes that "energy independence" itself is a contested term. The US became a net oil products exporter in Dec 2018 and a sustained net crude exporter in Jan 2020 — but still imported crude oil from Canada and OPEC nations simultaneously (for refinery mix reasons). The underlying trend (shale revolution) began under Obama. Trump accelerated it. The KEPT verdict is defensible for a book grading political promises against political benchmarks, but a petroleum economist might call it PARTIAL.
