## Tricky cases worth flagging

### 1. Promise #33 — "Protect Pre-Existing Conditions" (YOU_DECIDE verdict with direct debate quote)

This is the only batch 2 promise where the verbatim quote is directly fetched from debates.org but the promise is legitimately paradoxical. Trump said "we will be able to keep pre-existing" at the Oct 9 debate while simultaneously campaigning to repeal the ACA (which is the source of those protections) and later joining the Texas v. Azar lawsuit seeking to invalidate the entire ACA. The quote is real; the promise was self-contradictory. The book's YOU_DECIDE verdict is the honest answer. Cite the Oct 9 debate for the promise; cite SCOTUS's 7-2 ruling in *California v. Texas* (June 2021) for the outcome.

### 2. Promise #35 — "No Cuts to Medicare" (WEAKLY VERIFIED, YOU_DECIDE verdict)

The original source quote ("I'm not going to cut... Medicare or Medicaid") is from Oct 2015 CBS News interview and is widely attributed but not directly fetchable. More importantly, every Trump budget proposal (FY2018–FY2021) included $500B–$846B in Medicare spending reductions over 10 years (via provider payment cuts, not beneficiary cuts). Whether these budget proposals constitute "cuts to Medicare" is the entire definitional dispute. The book's YOU_DECIDE framing is correct, but the sourcing is harder than Social Security because the budget proposals are well-documented at OMB and CBO while the original promise is harder to pin to a single fetchable primary source.

### 3. Promise #28 — "Eliminate Federal Debt in 8 Years" (VERIFIED via PolitiFact, but original interview behind paywall)

The verbatim quote ("We've got to get rid of the $19 trillion in debt... over a period of eight years") is confirmed by PolitiFact but originally from a Washington Post / Bob Woodward & Robert Costa interview (April 2, 2016). This interview is behind the WaPo paywall and not directly fetchable. PolitiFact's page provides the quote without the original source details. The promise is real and well-documented, but the cleanest primary source URL would be the WaPo interview (paywalled). Recommend citing PolitiFact as the accessible corroborating source while noting the WaPo interview as the original context.
