## D. RISK NOTES (black-render prevention)

1. **Root cause of the 3 prior failures = dark briefs.** The earlier attempts logged "dusk living room" and "golden hour dome" (see `.external-costs.jsonl` higgsfield entries `hook-01-couch-atmosphere`, `context-03-capitol-atmosphere`). Dark/low-key prompts both black-rendered and would fail the YAVG≥70 luma gate. **Every brief above is explicitly high-key daylight 5600K, white/navy, YAVG target ≥80** — 10 points of headroom over the gate.
2. **Per-clip luma gate is the safety net.** Each generated AI clip must pass the Stage 24.5 brightness/black-frame QC gate (hard-fail) BEFORE it enters the master. Any clip that black-renders is rejected and rerolled (buffer is in the cost). No dark clip can reach the timeline — this is what makes adding AI back safe after the prior failures.
3. **No-real-face risk = eliminated by design.** All 9 shots are objects / environments / hands / silhouettes. The Flux seed stills are also faceless — no person seed means the model cannot drift into a real likeness. The Capitol-style façade (AI-08) is briefed as generic classical architecture, not a recognizable individual landmark portrait, to avoid both real-person and trademark/identity issues.
4. **Kling 2400-char motion-prompt limit.** Every motion prompt will be capped ≤2400 chars at word boundary (HTTP 422 above that). The briefs above are short enough that the full locked camera/lens/grade + photoreal + diegetic-audio block fits well under the cap.
5. **Text legibility preserved.** Because AI plates sit UNDER the existing Remotion TextSlate type (not replacing it), the navy/high-contrast brand copy is unaffected — no risk of the model garbling text, and no readable fake brand names on any mailer/envelope (all briefed blank).
6. **Duration mismatch.** Kling only outputs 5s/10s; longer beats hold/cross-dissolve the 10s plate under the longer card. No clip is stretched past its native length in a way that judders.
