This was the most expensive Senate race in the country. The biggest checks lost.

$110 million in outside money poured into one Texas seat. Most of it came from two billionaires. One super PAC spent $39.3 million to beat a Republican named Ken Paxton. Paxton won anyway — about 64%. The money lost. So we walked the receipts. Public filings, who paid, and why.

In this cut:

— John Nau, the Houston beer billionaire who gave $7.9M to a pro-Cornyn super PAC. He was Cornyn's finance chairman. The PAC spent $39.3M against Paxton. It did not work.

— Reid Hoffman, the LinkedIn co-founder, gave $1.5M from California to a super PAC backing James Talarico. That PAC spent $8.95M against Jasmine Crockett. Talarico wrote bills on social media and AI fakes — which is why the interest reaches Silicon Valley. There's no Senate vote yet, so what Hoffman gets back is still an open question.

— The disguised money: $5.5M reached the pro-Cornyn PAC through a shell called "Ohio Works." $3.75M reached the pro-Talarico PAC through "Government That Works." The original donors stay hidden. It's legal. Both sides do it.
