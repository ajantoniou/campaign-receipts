# SEALED-001 — Seven-persona validation pass

**Asset:** `companies/campaign-receipts/public/shorts/sealed-001-aipac-iran-deal.mp4`
**Specs:** 39.7s, 13 cuts, current viral-score 6/10
**Question:** Does this short pass the new 6th-grade-explainer + shock-AND-insight bar?
**Date:** 2026-05-19 (filename retains the originally-requested 2026-05-20 stamp)

Each persona files a ~100-word critique. Lay-viewer goes last; her
verdict is binding.

---

## 1. Political Journalist — VERDICT: CONDITIONAL

The factual scaffolding is defensible — Iran-deal withdrawal date,
embassy-move date, EO date are all real and survive a hostile read. But
the short uses "EO 13899" at 0:14 as if the viewer knows what an
executive-order number means. To Cincinnati Mom, that's an alphanumeric
string and she disengages. Either drop the number entirely and use the
plain-English consequence ("expanded the federal definition of
antisemitism — December 2019"), or keep the number as a small bottom-
corner credential while the highlighted phrase carries the meaning.
Defensibility AND comprehensibility — both required. Right now only one
clears.

---

## 2. Layperson-Attention Guru — VERDICT: CONDITIONAL

Hook at 0:00 — "$82 million bought 3 Trump promises" — is stakes + shock.
That's two out of three. The missing piece is the **insight promise** —
the phrase that tells the tired 38-year-old she's going to LEARN
something she can repeat, not just feel angrier. Six-word fix: "$82
million. 3 promises. Here's exactly how it worked." The phrase "how it
worked" is the insight promise. Without it, the short delivers shock,
which the founder explicitly said is not enough. With it, retention
past 3 seconds climbs and share rate roughly doubles in this lane.

---

## 3. Bestseller Sales Author — VERDICT: CONDITIONAL

The CTA at 0:27 — "SEALED2016.COM — 145 promises. Every receipt." —
reads as **database access**, which to Cincinnati Mom is *homework.* She
just finished a 40-second video; she doesn't want to open a spreadsheet
on Saturday. Rewrite as story-completion: "Want the full story of how
$82M became 3 policy wins? sealed2016.com." Now she's clicking to
*finish a story she's already invested in*, not to do research. Insider
voice is otherwise absent — the script avoids jargon-flexing. Good. Fix
the CTA and this becomes a book-funnel asset, not just a viral artifact.

---

## 4. Design Guru — VERDICT: CONDITIONAL

The document reveal at 0:14 centers "EO 13899" as the focal point. Wrong
focal point. Re-comp: the highlighted phrase on the document should be
"**expanded federal definition of antisemitism**" in a civic-red box,
with "EO 13899 — Dec 2019" reduced to a small bottom-corner credential.
The viewer now sees the *consequence*, not the citation. Also: Adelson's
face appears at 0:19 for under 2 seconds. The face IS the receipt —
hold him for 3+ seconds with the $0 → $82M count-up running over the
lower third so the viewer encodes face-with-money. Stamps and color
grade are correct.

---

## 5. YouTube Shorts Influencer — VERDICT: CONDITIONAL

Hook is half-right (stakes + shock, missing insight). Pacing holds — 13
cuts in 39.7s averages ~3s per cut, acceptable for the lay-explainer
lane. But the short is 5-7 seconds too long; lay-explainer Shorts retain
better at 30-33s than at 39.7s, especially when the CTA reads as
homework. Two specific fixes: (a) add "Here's how it worked" or
equivalent insight promise to the hook, (b) trim 5-7 seconds out of the
middle by collapsing two of the three receipt-beats into a tighter
sequence. Also: kill any unglossed acronym in the first 10 seconds.

---

## 6. Lay-Explainer Video Editor — VERDICT: CONDITIONAL

The short shows the WHAT (three promises kept) but under-shows the WHY
(why $82M to those three specific promises mattered to one donor). The
buyer reveal at 0:19 needs to hold Adelson's face for 3+ seconds with
the count-up live, AND needs one visual stake the layperson recognizes:
ideally a side-by-side of "what Adelson asked for publicly (2015-2016
op-eds, donor letters)" → "what Trump delivered (the three dated
actions)." Without that comparison beat, the viewer sees correlation,
not causation, and the insight is incomplete. Add 2-3 seconds of
ask-vs-delivered overlay before the verdict stamp.

---

## 7. The Lay Viewer (Sarah, 38, Cincinnati) — VERDICT: CONDITIONAL → NO (as currently cut)

Honestly? I'd watch the first 5 seconds because $82 million is a lot of
money and I want to know what happened. But around 0:14 something
flashes — "EO" something — and I don't know what that means, so I start
to wonder if this is for people smarter than me, and I scroll. If I'd
made it to the end, the website at the bottom looks like a research
project, not something I'd open. I wouldn't share this with my brother-
in-law as it stands — he'd say "yeah, politicians take money, what
else is new?" because I couldn't tell him *the specific thing I learned.*
Fix the acronym moment and give me one sentence I can repeat at dinner,
and I'd send it to him tonight.

---

## Consensus verdict

**CONDITIONAL — does not currently pass the new bar. Three required fixes
to ship:**

1. **Add the insight promise to the hook (0:00-0:03).** "$82 million. 3
   promises. Here's exactly how it worked." Six words. Largest single
   lift in retention + share rate.

2. **Replace "EO 13899" as the document focal point (0:14).** Highlight
   the plain-English consequence ("expanded federal definition of
   antisemitism") in a civic-red box; EO number stays as small bottom-
   corner credential. Kills the swipe-away moment Sarah identified.

3. **Rewrite the CTA (0:27-end) from database-access to story-completion.**
   "Want the full story of how $82M became 3 policy wins? sealed2016.com."
   Converts the short from a viral artifact into a book-funnel asset.

**Secondary fixes** (nice-to-have, not blocking): hold Adelson's face
3+ seconds at 0:19 with $0→$82M count-up; add 2-3s ask-vs-delivered
overlay before the verdict; trim 5-7s out of the middle to land at
~32-34s total.

With the three required fixes applied, this short passes the
6th-grade-explainer + shock-AND-insight bar. Without them, it's a 6/10
viral artifact that produces watches but not shares-with-context — which
is the founder's actual measure of success.
