---

## Scene 1 — Hook (0:00–0:12)

**VO:** "He told crowds that Washington was owned by lobbyists and donors, and that he would drain the swamp. This chapter is the receipt for what actually happened to that promise once he had the keys to the building."

---

## Scene 2 — The promise (0:12–0:35)

**VO:** "It started on June sixteenth, twenty fifteen, at Trump Tower. Politicians are almost completely controlled by lobbyists, donors, and special interests, he said — and we will defeat the special interests together. That line was not a side issue; it was the emotional core of the campaign."

---

## Scene 3 — Day-eight pledge (0:35–1:05)

**VO:** "Eight days into office he put something on paper. Executive Order thirteen seven seventy — the Ethics Pledge. Five-year ban on officials becoming lobbyists. Lifetime ban on lobbying for foreign governments. It hit the Federal Register on January twenty-eighth, twenty seventeen. For a moment it looked like the trail talk might become rule."

---

## Scene 4 — What voters expected (1:05–1:30)

**VO:** "In plain English, voters heard this: insiders would not cash out the minute they left government, and foreign governments could not buy revolving-door access. That is what drain the swamp sounded like on the trail — not a slogan on a hat, but a constraint on power."

---

## Scene 5 — The money got bigger (1:30–2:00)

**VO:** "Then the money tells a different story. Total lobbying spending in Washington rose from three-point-one-five billion dollars in twenty sixteen to three-point-five-three billion in twenty twenty — while the ethics pledge was still on the books. The swamp did not shrink on the spreadsheets that matter. It grew."

---

## Scene 6 — Revoked on the way out (2:00–2:35)

**VO:** "On January nineteenth, twenty twenty-one, he signed Executive Order thirteen nine eighty-three. The pledge was revoked effective January twentieth — his last day in office. Officials could lobby again immediately, and several did. So the signature that looked tough in week one did not survive the exit."

---

## Scene 7 — Not alone on donors (2:35–3:05)

**VO:** "Fair note before the verdict: big money exists on both sides. Soros, Bloomberg, Steyer, Saban, Hoffman — all gave huge sums to Democrats. This chapter still shows a pattern in the book: promises that required fighting donor power often broke, while promises that aligned with donor asks often got kept. We are not grading one party's donors — we are grading whether the swamp actually drained."

---

## Scene 8 — Chapter verdict (3:05–3:35)

**VO:** "Thirteen of eighteen drain-the-swamp promises in the book are graded broken. The ethics pledge was the one tangible action — and it did not survive the exit. Verdict for the chapter headline: BROKEN. This was not draining the swamp. It was privatizing it."

---

## Scene 9 — CTA (3:35–3:55)

**VO:** "One hundred forty-five promises. Same standard. Same receipts. SEALED twenty sixteen dot com. New receipts drop when we find them."
