## Scene 1 — Hook (0:00–0:12)

He promised the line would move one way.

One paper moved it the other.

A campus where you could say more. That was the promise.

Tonight, the order that made it less.

---

## Scene 2 — The promise, in plain words (0:12–0:45)

[EMPATHY BEAT 1 — HOOK-ACK]

You wanted free speech on campus protected. That's a fair thing to want.

On the 2016 trail, he ran on protecting free speech on campus.

Picture the school your kid is about to pay for. This is its rule book.

He said the line would move toward more speech. So you trusted the headline.

Then he signed something. Here's what it did.

## Scene 3 — Meet the definition. It's just a guide. (0:45–1:15)

First, meet a definition.

In 2016, a group called the I-H-R-A wrote down what antisemitism means.

In other words, they made a guide. It has examples that help people spot it.

On its own, that guide forced no school to do anything. It was a description.

So how did a guide get teeth?

---

## Scene 4 — December 11, 2019. The order. (1:15–1:55)

December eleventh, twenty nineteen. He signed Executive Order thirteen eight ninety-nine.

In other words, he told the government to use that guide. They would use it when they check schools.

The guide stopped being just a guide. The date is when the change happened.

Here is the careful part. The order itself calls the guide "non-legally binding."

So the same paper that picks up the guide admits one thing. The guide binds no one alone.

## Scene 5 — Title VI is the lever (1:55–2:30)

Now meet the lever. It's called Title Six.

In other words, it's the law. It lets Washington pull a school's federal money.

After the order, the guide's examples could feed a Title Six complaint.

The student says the same sentence as before. Same words. Same argument.

But the rule that sentence runs into just changed. And nobody voted on that.

---

## Scene 6 — Who this is for (2:30–2:55)

[EMPATHY BEAT 2 — WHY-THIS-MATTERS]

Think of the freshman walking onto campus this fall.

She didn't sign the order. She didn't write the guide.

She just showed up where the line had already moved.

The order widened nothing for her. It moved where her speech could go.

---

## Scene 7 — This is not old. Watch it run today. (2:55–3:35)

This is not a 2019 history lesson. Watch the same machine run today.

In July twenty twenty-five, Columbia University adopted that same I-H-R-A guide.

In other words, the school built the definition into how it handles complaints.

It did this under a deal. The deal restored federal money the government had cut.

That's the move again. A guide becomes a rule. A definition gets the lever.

And in March twenty twenty-five, the government warned sixty schools at once.

In other words, sixty campuses got letters about the same Title Six lever.

## Scene 8 — One act, two ledgers (3:35–4:00)

Here's the part the headline skipped.

The SEALED paper trail logs this same order as a kept promise.

It sits in a donor ledger. The donor is a lobby called AY-pack — A-I-P-A-C.

In other words, a Washington group that pushes for laws backing Israel.

In that ledger, the order is Priority Three. The note reads "Done."

So one act sits on two ledgers. It kept the donor's promise.

By our own audit, it broke the voter's. He promised wider. The order went narrower.

## Scene 9 — Verdict + CTA (4:00–4:25)

[EMPATHY BEAT 3 — COLD-CLOSE WARMTH]

Here's what this order taught us.

A promise about speech is only as wide as the rule behind it.

So next time someone says "protect free speech," ask two things.

Protect it for whom. And where is the line.

He promised to widen the debate. The order narrowed it.

By our own audit, that promise is BROKEN.

He promised to protect campus speech. Then one order drew the line around one kind of speech. Would you count that promise as kept?
