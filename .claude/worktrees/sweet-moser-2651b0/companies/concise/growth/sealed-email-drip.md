## Campaign Architecture

### Narrative Wedge
Each email frames a single quote within the "promises before the deals" narrative:
- **2016 Promise:** The original statement from the campaign
- **Context:** Why this promise mattered to voters
- **The Question:** Did the presidency deliver, pivot, or abandon this principle?

This positioning allows readers to form their own conclusions without explicit claims about policy success/failure.

### Email Structure (Template)

```
Email [#] | Week [#], [Day]

Subject A: [Direct question or statement about promise]
Subject B: [Alternative framing or broader context]

Quote: "[Exact text from source PDF]" (p. [page number])

Framing: [1-2 sentences connecting quote to campaign identity and "before the deals" moment]

CTA: [Rotating: Book $22 | Bundle $27 | Social share]
```

### CTA Rotation Strategy
**3-message cycle** (repeats every 3 emails):
- **Message 1 (CTA A):** Direct book sale — "Buy SEALED for $22"
- **Message 2 (CTA B):** Bundle upsell — "Get SEALED + 2 companion books for $27"
- **Message 3 (CTA C):** Social amplification — "Share this quote on social media"

**Rationale:** Rotates between direct conversion (email 1, 4, 7, 10...), upsell/bundling (email 2, 5, 8...), and viral/organic growth (email 3, 6, 9...).

## Compliance Framework

### Hard Constraints (Non-Negotiable)
- ✓ **Faceless/pseudonymous** — No founder identity, face, or voice revealed
- ✓ **All quotes traceable** — Every quote includes PDF page number
- ✓ **No anti-Semitic framing** — Sensitive topics (Israel, profiling, Muslims) framed neutrally
- ✓ **No health claims** — No "this will boost your health" or vaccine statements
- ✓ **No securities advice** — No "this will make you money" investment claims

### Framing Approach
Each email positions quotes within the "before the deals" narrative:
- Opens with 2016 campaign context
- States the promise directly
- Closes with open question (did presidency deliver?)
- **Never claims:** policy success, failure, or moral judgment
- **Always allows:** readers to form their own conclusions

### Sample Sensitive Topic Framing (Healthcare Section)

**Bad framing (violates criteria):**
"Trump promised to dismantle Obamacare and replace it with a better system. Here's proof he was right to attack the law."

**Good framing (meets criteria):**
"In 2016, Trump campaigned on healthcare reform. This quote shows his position before he took office. What actually happened to his healthcare agenda in the presidency?"
