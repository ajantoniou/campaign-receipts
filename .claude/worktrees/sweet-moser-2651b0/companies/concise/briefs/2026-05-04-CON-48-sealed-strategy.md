## Q1: One Book vs Two Books

### Recommendation: ONE BOOK (2016-only)

**Rationale:**

1. **Speed to first dollar:** 2016 material already exists (Grabit Nation PDF). Adding 2024 analysis doubles research time before ANY revenue validation.

2. **Market test clarity:** One proposition = clean signal. If "secret pre-capture promises" resonates, we see it in Book 1 sales. If it flops, we didn't waste time on Book 2.

3. **PDF economics:** $9.99-19.99 direct-sale PDFs have near-zero marginal cost. Better to ship Book 1 at $14.99 in 2 weeks than wait 6 weeks for a $24.99 mega-book that may not sell at all.

4. **Founder's "many shots" strategy:** Founder cited indie hacker with 35 apps where only 4 profitable. Book 1 is Shot #1. Book 2 is Shot #2 AFTER we know Shot #1 hit.

5. **Marketing simplicity:** "Here's what Trump promised BEFORE the lobbies bought him" is a single story. Adding "and here's 2024" muddies the core emotional hook — nostalgia for uncorrupted intent.

**Book 2 angle IF Book 1 sells:**

- **Title:** "SEALED 2024: The Second Capture"
- **Hook:** "He did it again. Compare 2024 pre-election promises to 2025 policy reversals. See the pattern."
- **Unique value:** longitudinal analysis — same methodology, different election cycle, PROVES the foreign-lobby thesis isn't partisan nostalgia but a repeating structural dynamic.

**Kill criteria for Book 2:** If Book 1 sells <50 copies in 60 days, don't build Book 2. Pivot to a different Concise title.

## Q2: Promise-Grading Framework

### Methodology

**Source hierarchy (most authoritative first):**

1. **Primary sources:** Actual Trump speeches, rally transcripts, debate transcripts, official campaign docs from 2015-2016 pre-nomination period.
2. **Legislative record:** Bills signed, executive orders issued, appointments made (verifiable actions).
3. **Investigative journalism:** ProPublica, OpenSecrets, FactCheck.org for lobby spending timelines and policy reversals.
4. **Excluded:** Op-eds, partisan think-tank "scorecards," social media hot takes.

**Grading scale:**

- **KEPT:** Promise fulfilled with legislation/EO/verifiable action matching the original commitment.
- **BROKEN:** Explicit reversal OR inaction when action was promised AND feasible.
- **PARTIAL:** Movement toward promise but incomplete (e.g., "Build the wall" → some wall built, not 2,000 miles).
- **BLOCKED:** External obstruction (Congress, courts) prevented fulfillment despite presidential effort.
- **MOOT:** Circumstances changed rendering promise irrelevant (e.g., ISIS territorial collapse).

**Example promises to grade (need actual research to complete):**

| # | Promise (2015-2016) | Grade | Evidence |
|---|---------------------|-------|----------|
| 1 | "Drain the swamp" — ban lobbyists from admin | BROKEN | Hired multiple Goldman Sachs execs, telecom lobbyists (cite: ProPublica tracker) |
| 2 | "Repeal and replace Obamacare" | BLOCKED | McCain thumbs-down vote July 2017 prevented repeal |
| 3 | "Move US Embassy to Jerusalem" | KEPT | EO Dec 2017, embassy opened May 2018 |
| 4 | "Ban Muslims from entering US" | PARTIAL | Travel ban for 7 countries (not all Muslim-majority), upheld by SCOTUS |
| 5 | "Make Mexico pay for the wall" | BROKEN | Mexico paid $0, US taxpayers funded partial construction |
| ... | (20 more promises TBD) | | |

**To complete this:** Head of Growth or Research role needs to:

## Appendix: Why Not A Comparison Book?

**"Why not both 2016 AND 2024 promises in one book?"**

Because the 2024 election JUST happened (Nov 2024) and we're only 6 months into his second term (writing this May 2026). There isn't enough LEGISLATIVE RECORD yet to grade 2024 promises fairly. A 2024 grading would be:

- Speculative ("he hasn't done X YET but might")
- Unfair (100 days is too early to judge most promises)
- Weaker marketing (no dramatic betrayal narrative if the betrayal is still in progress)

**Book 2 in 2028** (end of second term) will have the full record to compare. That's when "SEALED 2024" makes sense.

For NOW, 2016 has the complete story: promise → lobby money → reversal. That's the book that sells in 2026.
