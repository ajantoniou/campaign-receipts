## 1. Title/Subtitle Verdict

### Current Title (CEO-Locked)
**SEALED: The 2016 Promises — Before the Deals**

**Literary Agent Assessment: APPROVED with minor reservation**

This title works commercially. It delivers:
- **Archive intrigue** ("SEALED" = forbidden knowledge positioning)
- **Clear time anchor** (2016 = campaign era, not presidency)
- **The wedge** ("Before the Deals" = pre-lobbying baseline)
- **Platform safety** (no loaded trigger phrases)

**Minor reservation:** The em-dash may read as cluttered in small thumbnail views (Amazon, social media cards). Consider testing "SEALED: The 2016 Promises" as primary, with "Before the Deals" as subtitle on cover design.

### Alternative Title Recommendation from the 5 Candidates

If the founder reopens title discussion, **Title 4: "UNCOMPROMISED"** is the strongest alternative:

**UNCOMPROMISED: The Campaign Promises Trump Made Before the Lobbies**

**Why it works better commercially:**
- Single-word power (easier to remember, stronger brand)
- Emotional core front-loaded (validates reader frustration)
- "Before the Lobbies" is cleaner than "Before the Deals" (more specific wedge)
- Better SEO/social shareability ("uncompromised Trump" vs. "sealed Trump")

**Trade-off:** "UNCOMPROMISED" is more partisan-leaning, which may limit crossover appeal. "SEALED" is more neutral/archival.

**Verdict:** Stick with SEALED for platform safety and broader market. Reserve UNCOMPROMISED for a future edition or companion title.

## 2. Sales Page Copy Audit

### Section-by-Section Review

#### **Hero Section (Lines 74-82)**
**Current copy:**
> "What did Trump promise before the deals?"
> A historical archive of uncompromised policy vision.
> Campaign 2015-2016

**Assessment:** Strong. No changes needed.

---

#### **Wedge Explanation (Lines 88-96)**
**UNSAFE LINE (Line 92-93):**
> "...before post-presidency political and lobbying pressures reshaped his agenda."

**Problem:** "Post-presidency" is factually wrong. Trump's presidency was 2017-2021. This book covers *pre-presidency* (2015-2016 campaign). This line implies the book compares post-2021 actions to 2015-2016 promises, which is confusing.

**Replacement:**
> "Between 2015-2016, Trump articulated a specific policy vision on foreign affairs, trade, military strategy, healthcare, and economic transformation. This book archives that vision—word-for-word from debates, interviews, and campaign materials—before his presidency and subsequent political pressures reshaped the original agenda."

---

#### **Sample Quotes Section (Lines 105-131)**
**WEAK LINE (Lines 111-112):**
> "[The 2023-2026 reality: Middle East entanglements, foreign military commitments, lobbying pressures. This quote sits in the archive as the stated vision.]"

**Problem:** This editorial aside feels defensive and dilutes the "time capsule" positioning. Let the quote stand alone.

**Replacement:**
> "Campaign Interview, 2015"

(Remove the bracketed editorial. The wedge is already established in the intro section. Trust the reader to make the comparison.)

**Apply same fix to Lines 121-122 and Lines 130-131.** Remove bracketed editorials. Let the quotes breathe.

---

#### **Trust Signals Section (Lines 139-145)**
**UNSAFE LINE (Line 142):**
> "Published under pseudonym ("We The People") — author removed, message centered"

**Problem:** "Author removed" could imply the author was *fired* or *censored*, which invites conspiracy framing.

**Replacement:**
> "Published under the collective pseudonym 'We The People' to center the message, not the messenger"

---

#### **Email Capture CTA (Lines 151-163)**
**WEAK LINE (Line 161-162):**
> "We won't spam you. One email, the key document, plus quarterly policy deep-dives if you want them."

**Problem:** "Quarterly policy deep-dives" is vague and undermines the "one email" promise. This reads like bait-and-switch.

**Replacement:**
> "One email with the introduction and first 10 quotes. Unsubscribe anytime. Optional: quarterly updates on new Concise releases."

---

#### **Buy Section (Lines 168-183)**
**UNSAFE LINE (Lines 171-172):**
> "Limited Time: Add the "Foreign Influence Policy Tracking Spreadsheet" (tracks promise → reality for every quote) → $27 bundle"

**Problem:** "Foreign Influence" in the product name is too loaded for payment processors and platform moderation. This could trigger Stripe review or app store rejection.

**Replacement:**
> "Add the Policy Tracker Spreadsheet (tracks 2016 campaign promises vs. outcomes) → $27 bundle"

---

#### **FAQ Section (Lines 189-223)**
**STRONG.** No changes. The FAQ neutralizes objections cleanly without sounding defensive.

## 3. Back-Cover / Email Hook (3 Sentences)

### Concise Hook (Email Capture, Reddit, LemonSqueezy Description)

**Version A (Neutral/Archive Tone):**
> In 2015-2016, Trump promised a specific policy vision on trade, foreign affairs, healthcare, and military strategy. This book archives those promises—word-for-word from debates, interviews, and his own campaign books—before political deals and lobbying pressures reshaped the agenda. Read the original vision. Compare it to what happened next.

**Version B (Wedge-Forward Tone):**
> What did Trump promise before the foreign lobbies got involved? Between 2015-2016, he laid out a bold policy roadmap on trade, military strategy, and economic transformation. This archive captures the original, uncompromised vision—before the deals.

**Recommendation:** Use **Version A** for email capture and LemonSqueezy (broader appeal). Use **Version B** for Reddit comments in political subs (more engagement-optimized).

## 4. One-Paragraph Introduction (Book Interior)

### Draft Introduction (Concise Voice, No Verbosity)

On June 16, 2015, Donald Trump descended the escalator at Trump Tower and announced his candidacy for President of the United States. Over the next seventeen months, he articulated a policy vision that broke from decades of political consensus: renegotiate trade deals, confront NATO allies on funding, end foreign wars, and reject corporate lobbying. This book is not an analysis. It is an archive. Here are the promises Trump made—in his own words, from debates, rallies, and interviews—before he became President. No commentary. No spin. Just the record. Read what he said in 2015-2016. Then compare it to what happened next.
