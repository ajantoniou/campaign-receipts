# Brand / Design / Marketing — Trading Journal (Override)

**Inherits from:** `shared/personas/brand-design.md`

## Single brand voice

**Working name:** PROPOSE Saturday Day 1.

Suggested seeds:
- "EdgeJournal" / "PlutoJournal" / "EdgeBook" / "EdgeWise"
- "Pattern Edge" / "TradeBook Pro" / "Tradebook Edge"
- "Plutopath Edge" (leverage founder brand) / "Edgewise" / "Tradesage"
- Avoid: "guru," "alerts," "signals" (too commodity); "trader" alone
  (too generic); anything implying "calls" / "advice"
- Choose: data-driven, professional, evokes both journal AND edge

## Voice fingerprint

- **Data-driven** — backtest stats, methodology, research-grade tone
- **Sober** — no hype, no exclamation points, no "🚀" emoji
- **Educational** — explains the why, builds user understanding
- **Confident but humble** — "our model shows" not "we predict"
- **Anti-guru** — direct opposite of motivational guru aesthetic

## Visual aesthetic

- **Bloomberg Terminal vibes** but modern
- **Dark mode primary** (traders prefer dark UI)
- **Monochrome with one accent** — slate/charcoal + electric green
  (P&L positive) + red (P&L negative)
- **Typography:** monospace for data (JetBrains Mono, Fira Code), sans
  for prose (Inter)
- **Charts forward** — equity curves, win rate bars, drawdown plots
- **AVOID:** "stock chart with green arrows up," "lambo / Rolex
  imagery," "grinning trader at desk," motivational quote overlays

## Brand-product connection

The brand reinforces the data product positioning. Specifically:
- "Educational research-grade insights" — shown via methodology
  callouts
- Real backtest stats prominently displayed (not hidden)
- Disclaimer language is part of the brand, not an afterthought
- No "you'll get rich" promises in any creative

## Banned visuals/copy

### Visual
- Stock photos of "diverse traders smiling at multiple monitors"
- Generic stock chart green arrow up
- Lambo / Rolex / luxury imagery
- Cartoon bull / bear illustrations
- Motivational quote overlays
- Generic "candlestick chart" hero images

### Copy
- "Make $X/mo" / "Replace your salary"
- "Beat the market" / "Outperform the S&P"
- "Risk-free" / "Guaranteed returns"
- "Only [number] spots left" (fake scarcity)
- "Limited time offer" (when it's not)
- "Join thousands of traders profiting" (FTC violation)
- "I'll show you how I made $X" (FTC + advisor classification)
- "🚀" or "📈" emoji in marketing
- ALL CAPS HYPE WORDS

## Founder brand integration

The founder is a board-certified physician AND a real-money trader.
This is unusual + credible.

Brand can leverage:
- "Built by a physician trader" framing
- "Same patterns I trade with my own money"
- "Plutopath" as parent brand reference (but don't expose Plutopath
  internals — protect the edge)

Brand should NOT:
- Make founder the central marketing figure (founder ≠ guru)
- Use founder's face in primary marketing (faceless brand for
  scalability)
- Imply founder personally signs trades for users (advisor classification)
